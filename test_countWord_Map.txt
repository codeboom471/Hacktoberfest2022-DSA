They covered the precious mahogany coffin with a brown amalgam of rocks, 
decomposed organisms, and weeds. It was my turn to take the shovel, 
but I felt too ashamed to dutifully send her off 
when I had not properly said goodbye. I refused to throw dirt on her. 
I refused to let go of my grandmother, to accept a death I had not seen coming, 
to believe that an illness could not only interrupt, but steal a beloved life.
